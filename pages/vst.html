Volvo
Studio Tokyo Explorer

We were approached with a task of inventing an AR experience that could showcase the latest features of Volvoâ€™s new line of electric cars in a creative and playful way to appeal Volvo as a brand towards Japanese Millenials and Gen-Z.

The experience would showcase upon the reopening of the new Volvo Tokyo flagship showroom in Aoyama Tokyo.

We gathered moodboards of any relevant visualisations that would look beautiful in the studio space - we particularly looked at procedural waveform art. One of our concepts took the form of the Northern lights as inspiration for the AR space and experimented with artistic visualisations similar to nordic art, soundwaves and chalk drawings.

I was also responsible for helping design the trivia cards that appear in the AR space. I looked at website illustrations and examples of zines seen in my local japanese-style cafes for inspiration - particularly Brutus and Popeyes.

The final illustration style I knew had to align with the overall UI style we crafted for the experience while also allowing for playful representations. One of my favourite trivia illustrations was the the infamous Swedish smelly dish. The client loved and gave a laugh at the illustration.
